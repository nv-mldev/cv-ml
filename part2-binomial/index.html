Part 2: The Binomial Distribution — Counting Successes

DhvaniAI

Intuition¶

If a Bernoulli trial is one coin flip, the Binomial distribution answers: “If I flip the coin nnn times, how many heads will I get?”

In CV terms:

  • In a 10×10 image patch, if each pixel independently has probability p=0.3p=0.3p=0.3 of being an edge pixel, how many edge pixels kkk will we find?

  • In feature matching, if we test nnn descriptor pairs and each has probability ppp of being a true match, how many true matches kkk do we expect?

  • In sensor terms: nnn photons arrive, each with probability ppp of being detected — how many electrons kkk will we collect?

The Math¶

P(k∣n,p)=(nk)pk(1−p)n−kP(k \mid n, p) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}P(k∣n,p)=(kn​)pk(1−p)n−k
(1)

where (nk)=n!k!(n−k)!\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}(kn​)=k!(n−k)!n!​ is the number of ways to choose which kkk out of nnn trials succeed.

Mean: E[k]=npE[k] = npE[k]=np Variance: Var(k)=np(1−p)\text{Var}(k) = np(1-p)Var(k)=np(1−p)

Let’s break this formula apart piece by piece:

  • (nk)\binom{n}{k}(kn​) — how many ways can exactly kkk trials succeed out of nnn total?

  • pkp^kpk — probability that exactly those kkk trials all succeed

  • (1−p)n−k(1-p)^{n-k}(1−p)n−k — probability that all remaining trials fail

  • Multiply all three: total probability of getting exactly kkk successes

Visualizing the Binomial: What Does the Distribution Look Like?¶

The shape depends on nnn (number of trials) and ppp (success probability).

  • Increasing nnn (fix ppp): the distribution widens and becomes more bell-shaped. This is the first hint of the Normal approximation and the CLT.

  • Varying ppp (fix nnn): the peak shifts. The distribution is most symmetric when p=0.5p = 0.5p=0.5; it becomes skewed as ppp approaches 0 or 1.

Simulation: Repeated Exposures¶

Let’s simulate what happens when we take many exposures of the same scene. Each exposure is an independent set of nnn Bernoulli trials.

Why simulate when we have the formula? Simulation validates the theory and builds intuition. If simulation and formula disagree, one of them is wrong — this is a powerful debugging technique.

What to look for in the simulation vs PMF plot: the simulation bars should closely hug the theory line. Small residual differences are expected — they shrink as the number of simulations increases (also a CLT effect).
